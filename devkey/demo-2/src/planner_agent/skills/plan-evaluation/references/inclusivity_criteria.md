# Inclusivity Criteria for Marathon Route Planning

## Grading Formula

The inclusivity grade combines three factors:

```
combined_score = (diversity_score × 0.4) + (coverage_ratio × 0.3) + (underserved_coverage × 0.3)
```

| Combined Score | Grade | Assessment |
|---------------|-------|------------|
| >= 0.70 | A | Excellent diversity and community coverage |
| >= 0.55 | B | Good representation, minor improvements possible |
| >= 0.40 | C | Adequate but significant gaps in coverage |
| >= 0.25 | D | Poor inclusivity, route modifications needed |
| < 0.25 | F | Failing — route excludes most communities |

## Underserved Neighborhoods

Neighborhoods with median income below the city median ($42,000):
- East Las Vegas ($35,000)
- North Las Vegas ($38,000)
- Historic Westside ($28,000)
- UNLV Area ($32,000)
- Downtown ($42,000)

## Diversity Index Benchmarks

- **> 0.80**: Highly diverse community
- **0.65 - 0.80**: Moderately diverse
- **< 0.65**: Less diverse, may need engagement outreach

## Target Coverage

A 26.2-mile marathon should engage at least 5-6 distinct neighborhoods
to demonstrate strong community representation.
