# Economic Impact Model

## Visitor Multiplier

Each marathon participant brings approximately **2.5 additional visitors**:
- Spouse/partner: 0.7
- Family members: 0.8
- Friends: 0.5
- Spectators (unrelated): 0.5

## Sector Impact Formulas

### Hospitality
```
revenue = total_visitors × 0.6 × $150/night × event_duration_days
```
- 60% of visitors stay in paid accommodation
- Average nightly rate: $150 (varies by city)
- Hotels within 5 miles of course benefit most

### Food & Dining
```
revenue = total_visitors × $45/day × event_duration_days
```
- All visitors eat out during event
- $45 average daily food spend
- Includes restaurants, cafes, fast food, food trucks

### Retail
```
revenue = total_visitors × $25/day × event_duration_days × 0.3
```
- Only 30% engage in meaningful retail shopping
- Running specialty stores see highest lift
- Some stores on closed roads lose foot traffic (mixed impact)

### Transportation
```
revenue = total_visitors × $35/day × event_duration_days
```
- Rideshare, taxi, parking, public transit
- Higher per-person spend due to road closures
- Airport transfers for out-of-town visitors

### Tourism
```
revenue = total_visitors × 0.3 × $75/day × event_duration_days
```
- 30% of visitors participate in tourism activities
- Museums, shows, attractions, tours
- Often extends stay beyond race day

## Economic Multiplier

The **1.6× multiplier** accounts for:
- Employee wages from increased business → consumer spending
- Supply chain effects (hotels buying more food, linens, etc.)
- Tax revenue → public spending
- Induced effects from increased economic activity

## Impact by Event Duration

| Duration | Direct Impact Multiplier |
|----------|------------------------|
| 1 day (race only) | 1.0× |
| 2 days (expo + race) | 1.8× |
| 3 days (expo + race + recovery) | 2.6× |
| 4+ days (festival format) | 3.2× |
